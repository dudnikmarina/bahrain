Trading history

Close relations were forged between Japan and Bahrain, when Japan received the first overseas shipment of oil from the Kingdom following its discovery in 1932. Our strong political and economic relationship resulted in goods and service trade valued at US$115 million in 2015.

Lifestyle, community and leisure

There are a growing number of Japanese nationals living in Bahrain, who, according to 2017 Export Insider Survey, enjoy the best expat lifestyle in the world. They add to a cosmopolitan cultural mix, and they find much that reminds them of home.

The Japanese Society Autumn Festival ‘Aki Matsuri’ offers food, performances and traditional dance

There is an authentic Japanese garden to enjoy in central Bahrain

Bahrain has many excellent Japanese restaurants, including Bushido, Maki Bahrain and Sato

The Japanese Film Festival is hosted annually

Travel & Visas

Emirates, Qatar and Etihad offer convenient connecting flights from Bahrain to Tokyo, with a flying time of approximately 12 hours.

Visas for Japanese nationals are available on arrival. There is also an option to apply for an e-visa prior to travelling, simplifying the entry process. Contact the Bahrain Embassy or Consulate for more details.

Leading Japanese companies have chosen to invest in Bahrain, including: Toyota, Mitsubishi, Nomura Securities, Daiwa Securities, Bank of Tokyo, Sumitomo Corporation, SBI Pharmaceuticals, KINTEETSU World Express
