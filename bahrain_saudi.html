Trading history

Bahrain and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia have a deep and longstanding friendship with bilateral relations starting officially in 1971. Strong economic and political ties have developed through our close proximity and connection via the King Fahd causeway since 1986.

Many expats live with their families in Bahrain and commute daily to Saudi Arabia, with over 26,000 people using the bridge every day. Plans to develop a second road and rail connection to Saudi Arabia in 2018 will connect Bahrain to the proposed US$200 billion Gulf rail network, improving trading connections and boosting inter-Gulf trade.

Bahrain and Saudi Arabia have signed nine key grant agreements worth US$533 billion and a dedicated centre has opened for Saudi investors, providing support for those seeking to operate in Bahrain.

Lifestyle, community and leisure

We have welcomed over 5,000 Saudi nationals, who, according to 2017 Export Insider Survey, enjoy the best expat lifestyle in the world. They add to a cosmopolitan cultural mix, and they find much that reminds them of home.

The two countries host frequent diplomatic and economic delegations and there are many partnerships between business and cultural organisations – including taking part in the Al Janadriyah festival, organised by Saudi Arabia

Of our 12.3 million visitors in 2016 - 70% came from the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

The role of Saudi Arabian Cultural attaché to Bahrain is one of the oldest in the Arab world and has successfully promoted educational and cultural endeavours

Travel & Visas

Gulf Air and Saudia offer multiple flights daily between Manama and all major Saudi cities. The flight from Manama to Riyadh takes about an hour. The 16-mile causeway connects the two countries and is open 24 hours a day.

Nationals of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia do not need a visa upon entering Bahrain.

Leading Saudi Arabia companies have invested in Bahrain, including: JGC Gulf International, The Arab Investment Company, Saudi National Commercial Bank, Saudi Arabian Insurance Company, Arab Petroleum Investment Corporation, Saudi Bin Laden Group
