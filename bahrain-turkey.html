Trading history

Bahrain and Turkey have had diplomatic relations since Turkey opened its Embassy in Bahrain in 1990.

Bahrain EDB identifies mutually beneficial economic interests, and in 2016, Bahrain-Turkish trade (excluding oil) was worth US$295 million.

Travel & Visas

Gulf Air, Turkish Airlines and Pegasus fly daily to Turkey with a flight time of 4 hours. With 21 flights a week between Bahrain and Turkey, it’s never been easier to travel to the Kingdom.

Visas for Turkish nationals are available on arrival. There is also an option to apply for an e-visa prior to travelling, simplifying the entry process. Contact the Bahrain Embassy or Consulate for more details.

There are currently 30 companies operating in Bahrain, of these, eight are prominent Turkish banks, including: Ziraat, Kuveyt Turk, Yapi ve Kredi Bankası A.S, Vakıfbank Turkiye Vakiflar Bankasi, Finansbank A.S, Kuwait Turkish Participation Bank Inc, Turkiye Halk Bankası A.S, Denizbank A.S, ING Bank A.S and Turkiye Is Bankası A.S
